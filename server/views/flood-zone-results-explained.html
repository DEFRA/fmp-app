Map data

The flood map for planning shows river and sea flooding data only. It doesn’t include other sources of flooding. It is for use in development planning and flood risk assessments.

The data in the map relates to the selected location and is not specific to any property within it. The map is updated regularly and is correct at the time of printing.

When you need a flood risk assessment

You'll only need to do a flood risk assessment as part of your planning application if your development is any of the following:

  • bigger than 1 hectare (ha)
  • in an area with critical drainage problems as notified by the Environment Agency
  • identified as being at increased flood risk in future by the local authority's strategic flood risk assessment
  • at risk from other sources of flooding (such as surface water or reservoirs) and its development would increase the vulnerability of its use (such as constructing an office on an undeveloped site or converting a shop to a dwelling)

Next steps

If you think your development needs a flood risk assessment, you should speak to a flood risk consultant to do this.

If you don’t think your development needs a flood risk assessment, you should download your flood map for planning that shows your flood zone and include it in your planning application.

Flood zones explained

Flood zones are based on how likely it is that a location will flood from rivers and the sea.

Flood zone 1

Locations have a low probability of flooding, some locations will need a flood risk assessment. Find out more about which developments need a flood risk assessment in flood zone 1.

Flood zone 2

Locations have a medium probability of flooding and will need a flood risk assessment.

Flood zone 3

Locations have a high probability of flooding and will need a flood risk assessment.

Your results explained

Your selected location is in flood zone 3. It is also in an area that has some protection from flood defences.

You'll need to do a flood risk assessment as part of your planning application.

Flood defences reduce the probability of flooding from a particular source (a river or the sea) but do not completely remove the risk. They can fail or a flood could happen which is bigger than the one the defence is designed to protect against.

Climate change will increase flood risk in future. This means the level of protection existing flood defences currently provide, will reduce over time. The site may also be at risk from other sources of flooding.
