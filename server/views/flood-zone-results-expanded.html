When you do and don't need a flood risk assessment

You'll only need to do a flood risk assessment as part of your planning application if your development is any of the following:

  • bigger than 1 hectare (ha)
  • in an area with critical drainage problems as notified by the Environment Agency
  • in an area that could be affected by sources of flooding other than rivers and the sea (such as surface water or reservoirs)
  • a change of use in development type to a more vulnerable category (for example from commercial to residential) and in an area that could be affected by sources of flooding other than rivers and the sea

Next steps

If you think your development needs a flood risk assessment, you should speak to a flood risk consultant to do this.

If you don’t think your development needs a flood risk assessment, you should download your flood zone map and include it in your planning application.

Flood zones explained

Flood zones determine how likely it is that a location will flood.

All locations in flood zones 2 and 3 need a flood risk assessment. Some locations in flood zone 1 need a flood risk assessment.

Flood zone Probability of flooding Assessment needed?
1 Low Some
2 Medium Yes
3 High Yes
