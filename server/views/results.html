Climate change: projected chance of flooding

This location is at risk from flooding from rivers or the sea when climate change is taken into account:

  • from rivers for the period 2070 to 2125
  • from the sea (tidal flooding) by the year 2125

We do not show the chance of flooding each year, or annual exceedance probability (AEP), for this dataset, as it is produced using a number of different source datasets.

Our rivers and sea information that is used to produce this dataset includes:

  • undefended
  • defended
  • other suitable data from third parties

What does 'defended' mean?
  • our modelling has taken into account the presence of existing flood defences
  • there is still a flood risk if the flood defences fail or if their design standard is exceeded (if the flood event is more than they are designed to cope with)
  • when considering climate change, we assume the presence of flood defences stay the same as present day
What does 'undefended' mean?
  • our modelling ignores the presence of existing flood defences

Flood zones with climate change are given to help you further investigate flood risk.

No data available

In this location, all or part of the site is included in a location where we hold limited flood risk information.

No data in this location does not mean there is no flood risk. Further assessment is needed to understand the flood risk in this location.

What does 'no data available' mean?

In some locations, we have chosen to remove datasets because of missing data, low confidence in the datasets or potential data inconsistencies.

The Environment Agency may still hold flood risk information that can be used to inform or prepare a flood risk assessment, which can be requested through the flood map for planning service.

'No data available' locations are currently under review.

As new models, data and information become available that meet our quality assurance standards, we will add these to the flood map for planning.
